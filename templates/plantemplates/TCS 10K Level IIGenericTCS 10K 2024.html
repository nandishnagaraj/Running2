Warm Up
Exercise Description
Light Jogging Start with a slow, easy jog for 5-10 minutes to increase your heart rate and warm up your muscles.
Leg Swings Swing each leg forward and backward and then side to side.
Arm Circles Rotate your arms in small and large circles.
Hip Circles Rotate your hips in a circular motion.
Acceleration Gliders Gradually increase your pace over a short distance, focusing on your running form.
Build-Ups Gradually increase your speed over 50-100 meters, reaching near-sprint speed without straining.
High Knees Stand tall and lift your knees towards your chest while jogging in place. Focus on keeping a quick and light rhythm.
Butt Kicks Jog in place while kicking your heels towards your glutes. Keep your knees pointed towards the ground.
Dynamic Lunges Step forward with one foot into a lunge position. Alternate legs, dynamically moving forward.
Skipping Skip in place, lifting your knees as high as comfortable. Engage your arms in a natural skipping motion.
A-Skips Lift your knee and drive it forward while hopping on the opposite foot. Emphasize driving the knee up towards your chest.
Bounding Take exaggerated, powerful strides while running. Focus on covering as much ground as possible with each stride.
Carioca Cross one foot over the other while moving laterally. Alternate the direction to engage different muscles.
Frankenstein Walks Extend your leg straight in front of you, aiming to touch your hand. Walk forward with alternating legs.
Ankle Rolls Lift one foot and rotate your ankle clockwise and then counterclockwise. Switch to the other foot.
Cool Down
Exercise Description
Slow Jogging Finish your run with a slow, easy jog to gradually decrease your heart rate and cool down your muscles.
Static Stretching Hold stretches for major muscle groups, including hamstrings, quadriceps, calves, and hip flexors. Hold each stretch for 15-30 seconds.
Deep Breathing Practice deep, diaphragmatic breathing to promote relaxation and reduce tension in your muscles.
Legs Up the Wall Lie on your back with your legs extended up against a wall. This helps promote blood circulation and reduces muscle fatigue.
Seated Forward Bend Sit with your legs extended in front of you and reach towards your toes, stretching your hamstrings and lower back.
Child's Pose Kneel on the ground, sit back on your heels, and stretch your arms forward. This stretches your back, shoulders, and hips.
Calf Stretch Stand near a wall, place one foot behind you, and lean forward to stretch your calf muscles.
Neck Stretch Gently tilt your head to each side, forward, and backward to release tension in your neck muscles.
Side Stretch Stand or sit and reach one arm overhead, leaning to the opposite side to stretch your lateral muscles.
Quadriceps Stretch Stand on one leg, bring your other foot towards your buttocks, and gently pull your foot towards your glutes to stretch your quadriceps.
